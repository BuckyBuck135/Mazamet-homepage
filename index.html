Visit Mazamet

Nature, history and gastronomy in the heart of southwest France.

Top three activities to do in Mazamet

Get on your bike

With both mountain roads and flat cycling paths, Mazamet is a cyclist's paradise.

Witness rich architectural heritage

Many gorgeous villas were built in Mazamet between 1850 and 1950, testimony to prosperous industrial activity.

Sample local delicacies

A trip to Mazamet is not complete without a taste of melsat or a slice of mouna.

Cycling

Mazamet is a great base from which to explore everything from UNESCO World Heritage sites to mountain lakes, vineyards to superb cycling countryside.

From fantastic road cycling (used on a number of occasions by the Tour de France) to the more sedate “Voie Verte” (a 65km cycle path using a former railway line) – Mazamet is a cyclist’s dream!

Explore the area

Architecture

Walking around town, you can not help but notice grand houses with exceptionnal architecture.

Built with the riches created from the wool and leather trade, these fine houses with different styles (neoclassical, palladian and south American style) are standing the test of time among camelias and chestnut trees.

Local delicacies

Mazamet is home to a number of typical culinary specialties. Whether you are a sweet tooth or more of a meat-lover, you will find something to satisfy your cravings.

Melsat is a thick sausage made with bread, eggs and pork meat. It is a speciality of the South of Tarn. It is usually enjoyed fried with a sunny-side up egg on toast.

Or give give the pumpet a go. Despite its funny name, pumpet is a lemonicious puff pastry, said to date back from the 8th century.
